Reading Module Overview (B2)

The ISE II Reading Module is part of the 2-hour Reading & Writing exam. It consists of 2 reading tasks with a total of 30 questions (15 questions per task). The tasks test your ability to understand complex texts, identify main ideas, extract specific information, and interpret meaning at B2 level.

Task 2: Multi-text Reading
20 mins

Read 4 texts (900 words total) and answer 15 questions

Key Differences from ISE I (B1):
  • Longer texts: 500 words (Task 1) vs. 400 words; 900 words (Task 2) vs. 700 words
  • More complex vocabulary: Academic and abstract language
  • More inference required: Understanding implied meaning and attitude
  • Gap-fill with word bank: Task 2 uses options from a word bank
  • Abstract topics: Society, values, environmental concerns, public figures
Important: You must complete both reading tasks before moving to the writing tasks (Task 3 and Task 4). The information from Task 2 is required for Task 3 (Reading into Writing), so read carefully and consider taking notes.

Task 1: Long Reading (20 minutes)
Task Overview: Read one complex text of approximately 500 words (divided into 5 paragraphs) and answer 15 questions testing different reading skills. The text is factual or descriptive and relates to one of the 10 subject areas.

Question Types Breakdown:

1 Questions 1-5: Title Matching

Match the correct title to each of the 5 paragraphs. You have 6 titles to choose from (one is a distractor). Tests understanding of main ideas.

Strategy: Look for topic sentences (usually first sentence of paragraph). Identify the main idea, not just specific details.
2 Questions 6-10: True Statements Selection

Choose 5 true statements from a list of 8. Three statements are false or not mentioned according to the text. Tests detailed comprehension and ability to distinguish fact from opinion.

Strategy: Scan for keywords from each statement. Check carefully - statements may use synonyms or paraphrase the text. Watch for extreme language ("always," "never") which is often false.
3 Questions 11-15: Sentence Completion (Gap-fill)

Complete sentences with exact words from the text (maximum 4 words per gap). Tests precise understanding and ability to locate specific information.

Example: "The author suggests that _______ policies are necessary to address climate change."
Answer from text: "comprehensive environmental"

Practical Example: Task 1 Reading Text

The Importance of Emotional Intelligence

Paragraph A
For many years, traditional education focused primarily on academic achievement, measuring success through test scores and grades. However, recent research has shown that emotional intelligence (EQ) may be equally important for long-term success. Emotional intelligence refers to the ability to recognise, understand, and manage our own emotions, as well as the emotions of others. This skill set includes empathy, self-awareness, and the ability to handle interpersonal relationships effectively.

Paragraph B
In the workplace, employees with high emotional intelligence often outperform their colleagues. They are better at working in teams, resolving conflicts, and adapting to change. A study conducted by a leading business school found that managers with high EQ scores were more likely to retain talented staff and maintain high productivity levels. These managers create positive working environments where employees feel valued and understood.

Paragraph C
Despite its importance, emotional intelligence is rarely taught in schools. Most curricula focus on academic subjects like mathematics and science, leaving little room for developing interpersonal skills. Some educators argue that there is not enough time in the school day to add another subject. However, critics point out that emotional intelligence can be integrated into existing subjects rather than taught separately.

Paragraph D
Developing emotional intelligence requires practice and self-reflection. One effective method is keeping a journal to track emotional responses to different situations. Another approach is seeking feedback from trusted friends or colleagues about how one's behaviour affects others. Meditation and mindfulness exercises can also help individuals become more aware of their emotional states and reactions.

Paragraph E
As artificial intelligence and automation continue to transform the workplace, emotional intelligence is becoming even more valuable. Machines can process data and perform calculations, but they cannot replicate human empathy or build meaningful relationships. Professionals who combine technical expertise with strong emotional skills will be best positioned for success in the future economy. Therefore, investing time in developing emotional intelligence is a wise decision for anyone seeking career advancement.

  • a) Emotional intelligence is more important than academic achievement for success.
  • b) Managers with high EQ are more likely to keep talented employees.
  • c) Emotional intelligence cannot be taught in schools.
  • d) Meditation can help develop emotional awareness.
  • e) Machines can replicate human empathy completely.
  • f) Journaling is one way to develop emotional intelligence.

Strategies for Task 1 Success:
  • ‚úì Skim first: Read quickly for general meaning before looking at questions
  • ‚úì Analyse paragraph structure: Topic sentences often contain main ideas for title matching
  • ‚úì Scan for keywords: Look for specific names, numbers, or phrases from questions

  • ‚úì Watch for synonyms: The text may use different words with similar meanings

Task 2: Multi-text Reading (20 minutes)
Task Overview: Read 4 short texts (approximately 900 words total) on the same theme. One text is typically an infographic (diagram, table, or chart with text). Answer 15 questions testing your ability to synthesize information across multiple sources.

Question Types Breakdown:

1 Questions 16-20: Text Matching

Match descriptions to the correct text (A, B, C, or D). Tests understanding of main idea, purpose, or tone of each text.

Example: "Which text presents statistical evidence?" ‚Üí Match to correct letter
Strategy: Read each text quickly to identify its main purpose and type (opinion, fact, advice, data).
2 Questions 21-25: True Statements Selection

Choose 5 true statements from 8 options based on information across all four texts. Requires synthesizing information from multiple sources.

Strategy: These questions may require information from 2 or more texts. Check all texts before deciding.
3 Questions 26-30: Summary Gap-fill with Word Bank

Complete summary notes using words from a provided word bank. The notes synthesize key information from all four texts. This is different from Task 1 - you choose from options, not from the text directly.

Example Word Bank: sustainable, economic, controversial, beneficial, problematic, essential, optional, widespread

"Many experts believe that _______ development is _______ for future generations."

Practical Example: Task 2 Texts (Simplified)

Theme: Remote Working

Text A - Opinion Article
Remote working has revolutionised the modern workplace. Employees report higher satisfaction levels and improved work-life balance. However, some managers struggle to maintain team cohesion when staff are dispersed. The key is finding the right balance between flexibility and structure.

Text B - Statistical Data (Infographic)
73% of remote workers report higher job satisfaction
45% of companies saw increased productivity
28% of employees feel isolated
60% of managers find team communication challenging

Text C - Expert Interview
Dr. Sarah Chen, workplace psychologist: "The shift to remote work isn't simply about location - it's about trust. Companies that succeed are those that focus on outcomes rather than hours worked. However, we must address the mental health implications of isolation."

Text D - Company Case Study
TechCorp implemented a hybrid model in 2022. Results after one year: employee retention increased by 35%, sick days decreased by 20%, but innovation metrics dropped slightly. The company addressed this by implementing virtual brainstorming sessions.

Sample Questions:

Text Matching Example:

Match the descriptions to the correct text (A, B, C, or D):

  • Presents numerical data about remote working trends
  • Describes a specific company's experience with hybrid work
  • Offers a professional opinion on psychological aspects
  • Discusses general advantages and challenges

Answers: B, D, C, A

Gap-fill with Word Bank Example:

Word Bank: satisfaction, isolation, productivity, retention, innovation, communication, flexibility, outcomes

Complete the summary:

  • Remote working has led to increased employee _______ and improved _______.
  • However, challenges include employee _______ and _______ difficulties.
  • Successful companies focus on _______ rather than monitoring hours.

Answers: satisfaction, productivity (or retention), isolation, communication, outcomes

Critical for Task 3: The information in these texts is used for Task 3 (Reading into Writing). Pay attention to:
  • Statistics and numerical data
  • Expert opinions and quotes
  • Contrasting viewpoints
  • Examples and case studies
  • Causes and effects

  • ‚úì Skim all texts first: Get an overview of what each text contains
  • ‚úì Identify text types: Is it opinion, data, advice, or narrative?
  • ‚úì Look for connections: How do the texts relate to each other?
  • ‚úì Note contradictions: Do any texts present opposing views?
  • ‚úì Word bank strategy: Read the summary first to understand context, then match words
  • ‚úì Take brief notes: Jot down main points from each text for Task 3
Reading Strategies for B2 Level

1. Skimming and Scanning

1 Skimming - Read quickly for general idea

Focus on: Title, first paragraph, topic sentences, last paragraph. Don't stop for unknown words.

2 Scanning - Look for specific information

Use keywords from questions. Look for names, dates, numbers, and specific terms.

2. Dealing with Unknown Vocabulary

Strategy How to Apply Example
Context clues Look at surrounding words and sentences "The arduous journey took 12 hours" ‚Üí arduous = difficult/long
Word formation Recognise prefixes, suffixes, roots "unsustainable" = un (not) + sustain (maintain) + able (can be)
Synonym recognition Look for words with similar meanings "happy, delighted, pleased" - all similar
Ignore if not essential Don't get stuck on one word If you understand the sentence without it, move on

3. Identifying Text Structure

Common B2 Text Structures:
  • Problem-Solution: Present issue ‚Üí Offer solutions ‚Üí Evaluate
  • Cause-Effect: Describe situation ‚Üí Explain reasons ‚Üí Show results
  • Compare-Contrast: Present two viewpoints ‚Üí Compare aspects ‚Üí Conclude
  • Argument-Evidence: State claim ‚Üí Provide proof ‚Üí Address counterarguments

4. Time Management (40 minutes total)

Activity Time Purpose
Task 1 - Skim text 2 minutes Get overall understanding
Task 1 - Answer questions 16 minutes Complete all 15 questions
Task 1 - Check answers 2 minutes Review uncertain answers
Task 2 - Skim all texts 3 minutes Understand each text's purpose
Task 2 - Answer questions 15 minutes Complete all 15 questions
Task 2 - Check & note key info 2 minutes Review and prepare for Task 3

5. Common Traps to Avoid

  • Matching exact words: The answer often uses synonyms, not the exact same words
  • Ignoring "not mentioned": Some statements may be true in reality but NOT in the text
  • Choosing the distractor: The extra title in Task 1 is designed to look correct
  • Spending too long on one question: Move on and return if time permits
  • Not checking word count in gap-fill: Maximum 4 words - check your answer fits

Reading Exam Checklist

Use this checklist before and during your reading exam:

Before the Exam:

  • I have practised skimming and scanning techniques
  • I can complete reading tasks within the time limit (40 minutes total)
  • I understand the difference between Task 1 and Task 2 gap-fill formats
  • I know how to identify topic sentences in paragraphs
  • I can recognise synonyms and paraphrasing
  • I understand how to use word banks effectively

During the Exam:

  • I skim the text first before reading questions
  • I check how many words are allowed in gap-fill answers
  • I look for the distractor in title matching questions
  • I verify that true statements are actually in the text (not just my knowledge)
  • I manage my time - 20 minutes per task
  • I note key information from Task 2 for use in Task 3
  • I check my answers if I have time remaining
