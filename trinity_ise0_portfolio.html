ISE Foundation Portfolio Guide

Three Tasks: Correspondence, Factual Writing & Creative Writing

What is the ISE Foundation Portfolio?

The Portfolio is one of three components of the ISE Foundation exam (A2 level). It consists of three pieces of written work that you prepare before the exam. The portfolio demonstrates your best writing ability and accounts for 20% of your total mark.

Key Facts:
  • You choose 3 tasks from 15 options (5 in each section)
  • You must complete one task from each section
  • You have 6-12 weeks to prepare (recommended)
  • You can use dictionaries, spell-checkers, and ask for teacher feedback
  • The examiner assesses your portfolio before your speaking exam
Important Rules:
  • Tasks must be from the current year's list (January-December)
  • Work must be your own — no copying or plagiarism
  • Teacher can give one piece of feedback per task only
  • You must include a word count for each task
  • Present tasks in order: Section 1, Section 2, Section 3
The Three Portfolio Sections
Section 1: Correspondence 40-60 words

Task types: Email, letter, postcard, note, memo

Focus: Personal communication with appropriate style and format

Weighting: 20% of portfolio mark

Section 2: Factual Writing 80-100 words

Task types: Article, report, review, instructions, directions

Focus: Conveying factual information clearly and accurately

Weighting: 40% of portfolio mark

Section 3: Creative/Descriptive Writing 80-100 words

Task types: Story, diary entry, description

Focus: Using language imaginatively to describe people, places, experiences

Weighting: 40% of portfolio mark

Assessment Criteria

Examiners assess your portfolio using one overall criterion: Task Fulfilment. This includes:

Factor What the Examiner Looks For
Communicative Aim Did you complete the task? Is the message clear?
Text Organisation Logical structure, paragraphs, connectors
Format & Register Correct layout (email vs letter), appropriate formality
Language Accuracy Grammar, vocabulary, spelling, punctuation
Effect on Reader Is it interesting? Appropriate tone?
High Accuracy Expected: Because you can use spell-checkers, dictionaries, and time to draft, the examiner expects a high level of accuracy in your portfolio. Mistakes will affect your grade more than in the exam room.
Section 1: Correspondence (40-60 words)

Task Types: Email, letter, postcard, note, memo

Word Count: 40-60 words (strictly enforced)

Time Suggested: 2-3 hours per draft

What You Need to Demonstrate:

  • Appropriate greeting and closing for the text type
  • Clear purpose (why are you writing?)
  • Basic information exchange
  • Simple and routine communication
Sample Task from Official List:
"You are on holiday with your family in the mountains. Write a postcard to a friend telling him where you are, what the weather is like and what you are doing."
Model Answer 1: Postcard (52 words)
Dear Maria, Greetings from the mountains! I'm here with my family for a week. The weather is fantastic — sunny and warm every day. Yesterday we went hiking and saw a beautiful lake. Today we're going to visit a small village nearby. The food is delicious! See you next week. Love, Anna
Why this is good:
  • Appropriate postcard format (greeting, message, closing)
  • Covers all three points: location, weather, activities
  • Simple past and going to for future
  • Within word count (52 words)
  • Friendly, informal tone suitable for a friend
Model Answer 2: Email to a Friend (58 words)
Hi Tom, Thanks for your email. I'm writing to tell you about my new job. I started last Monday and I really like it. My colleagues are friendly and the work is interesting. I work from 9 to 5 every day. Let's meet for coffee next weekend. Are you free on Saturday? Best wishes, Lucy
Language Features for Correspondence:
  • Greetings: Dear..., Hi..., Hello...
  • Closings: Love, Best wishes, Regards, See you soon
  • Opening phrases: Thanks for..., I'm writing to..., How are you?
  • Closing phrases: Write soon, Let's meet..., Give my love to...
Common Correspondence Tasks
Task Type Key Features Example Situation
Postcard Brief, chatty, no address needed Holiday greetings, short update
Informal email Hi/Hello, friendly tone, contractions To friends or family
Formal email/letter Dear Mr/Ms, polite language, no contractions To teacher, employer, official
Note Very short, often just message Leaving a message for someone
Memo To/From/Date/Subject format Workplace communication
Section 2: Factual Writing (80-100 words)

Task Types: Article, report, review, instructions, directions

Word Count: 80-100 words (strictly enforced)

Weighting: 40% of portfolio (most important section)

What You Need to Demonstrate:

  • Ability to convey factual information clearly
  • Logical organisation of information
  • Appropriate style for the text type
  • Use of sequencing (first, then, finally) for instructions
Sample Task from Official List:
"You are going on a camping trip with a group of friends. Write some instructions for your friends saying what clothes, food and equipment to bring."
Model Answer 1: Instructions (88 words)
Camping Trip Instructions Please bring the following items for our camping trip: Clothes: Bring warm clothes for the evening, a waterproof jacket and comfortable walking shoes. Don't forget your swimming costume! Food: We will cook together, but please bring snacks and drinks for yourself. Bring a bottle of water. Equipment: You need a sleeping bag and a torch. If you have a tent, please bring it. We also need one camping stove per group. See you on Saturday morning! Sam
Why this is good:
  • Clear title and introduction
  • Organised with bullet points/categories
  • Imperative verbs (bring, don't forget)
  • Covers all three required areas (clothes, food, equipment)
  • Appropriate word count (88 words)
Model Answer 2: Review (94 words)
Restaurant Review: Pizza Palace Last weekend, I visited Pizza Palace with my family. The restaurant is in the city centre, near the train station. The food was excellent. I ordered a margherita pizza and my brother had pasta. The portions were very big and everything was fresh. The prices were reasonable — about €15 per person. The waiter was friendly but the service was a bit slow because the restaurant was busy. The atmosphere was nice and the music wasn't too loud. I recommend this restaurant for families. I will definitely go back soon!
Language for Reviews:
  • Introduction: Last weekend..., I recently visited...
  • Positive: excellent, delicious, fantastic, reasonable prices, friendly staff
  • Negative: disappointing, expensive, slow service, too noisy
  • Recommendation: I recommend..., I suggest..., Perfect for families
Model Answer 3: Article (96 words)
How to Stay Healthy Do you want to feel better and have more energy? Here are three easy ways to stay healthy. First, eat plenty of fruit and vegetables. Try to eat five portions every day. Second, do regular exercise. You should walk or cycle for thirty minutes daily. Finally, drink lots of water and sleep for eight hours every night. Small changes can make a big difference. Start today and you will feel healthier tomorrow! What do you do to stay healthy? Send us your tips!
Article Features:
  • Catchy title
  • Engaging opening question
  • Sequencing words (First, Second, Finally)
  • Direct address to reader (you)
  • Conclusion with call to action
Section 3: Creative/Descriptive Writing (80-100 words)

Task Types: Story, diary entry, description (person, place, experience)

Word Count: 80-100 words (strictly enforced)

Weighting: 40% of portfolio

What You Need to Demonstrate:

  • Imaginative use of language
  • Descriptive vocabulary (adjectives, adverbs)
  • Ability to engage the reader
  • Past tenses for stories and diary entries
Sample Task from Official List:
"Write a description of your favourite hobby for a family magazine. Say when you started it, how often you do it and what special equipment you use."
Model Answer 1: Description of a Hobby (89 words)
My Favourite Hobby: Photography My favourite hobby is photography. I started taking photos three years ago when my dad gave me a camera for my birthday. I take pictures every weekend. I usually go to the park or the city centre to photograph nature and people. Sometimes I take photos of my friends and family at special events. I use a digital camera and my smartphone. I also have a tripod for steady shots. I love editing my photos on the computer and sharing them online. Photography helps me see the beauty in everyday life.
Why this is good:
  • Clear title
  • Past simple for when started
  • Present simple for routines (every weekend, usually)
  • Specific details (tripod, editing on computer)
  • Personal conclusion showing enjoyment
Model Answer 2: Story (95 words)
A Lucky Escape Last Saturday, I decided to go for a walk in the forest. The weather was beautiful and I wanted some fresh air. I walked for about an hour and then I realised I was lost. I couldn't see the path and my phone had no signal. I felt scared and worried. Suddenly, I heard a dog barking. I followed the sound and saw a man with his dog. He showed me the way back to the village. I was so relieved! I always take a map with me now.
Story Language:
  • Time markers: Last Saturday, Suddenly, Then, Finally
  • Past tenses: decided, was, realised, felt, heard
  • Adjectives for feelings: scared, worried, relieved, excited
  • Direct speech: Can you help me? (optional)
  • Conclusion: Lesson learned or happy ending
Model Answer 3: Diary Entry (86 words)
Saturday 15th June Today was the best day ever! I went to the amusement park with my best friends, Tom and Sara. We arrived at 10 o'clock and went on all the rides. My favourite was the roller coaster — it was terrifying but amazing! We ate hot dogs and ice cream for lunch. In the afternoon, we won prizes at the games stall. I felt tired but very happy when I got home. I want to go back again soon! Bedtime now. Good night!
Diary Features:
  • Date at the top
  • Personal pronouns (I, my)
  • Chronological order
  • Feelings and opinions
  • Informal style (exclamation marks, Bedtime now)
Step-by-Step Portfolio Writing Process

Step 1: Choose Your Tasks

From the official Trinity list (15 tasks total), select:

  • One from Section 1 (Correspondence)
  • One from Section 2 (Factual Writing)
  • One from Section 3 (Creative Writing)

Tip: Choose topics you know well and can write about personally.

Step 2: Plan Your Content

Before writing, make notes:

  • What is the purpose?
  • Who is the reader?
  • What points must I include?
  • What tense should I use?

Step 3: Write the First Draft

Don't worry about perfection. Focus on:

  • Including all required information
  • Organising ideas logically
  • Using appropriate layout

Step 4: Self-Edit

Check your work carefully:

  • Word count (use Word Count tool)
  • Spelling (use spell-checker)
  • Grammar (tenses, articles, prepositions)
  • Punctuation (capital letters, full stops, commas)
  • Task completion (all points covered?)
  • Format (correct layout for text type)

Step 5: Teacher Feedback

Give your draft to your teacher. They can provide one piece of feedback per task using the official feedback form. They cannot correct errors directly.

Step 6: Final Draft

Write your final version incorporating feedback. Check again for accuracy.

Grammar Checklist for A2 Level
Grammar Point Example Used In
Present Simple I work, She works, Do you like? Routines, facts
Present Continuous I am working, He is playing Now, temporary situations
Past Simple I visited, She went, Did you see? Stories, past events
Going to (future) I'm going to visit Plans, intentions
Can/Can't I can swim, She can't come Ability, possibility
Comparatives bigger, more expensive Comparisons
Imperatives Bring, Don't forget Instructions
Final Submission Checklist

Before handing in your portfolio, ensure you have:

  • Three tasks only (one from each section)
  • Tasks in correct order: Section 1, Section 2, Section 3
  • Word count written on each task
  • Portfolio Cover Sheet completed
  • Student Portfolio Feedback Forms attached (one per task)
  • Tasks are your own work (no copying)
  • No draft versions included
  • Presented in the official colour-coded wallet
Remember: The examiner will assess your portfolio before your speaking exam. You cannot change it after submission. Make sure it represents your best work!
