ISE Foundation Writing Guide

Master Task 3 (Reading into Writing) and Task 4 (Extended Writing)

ISE Foundation Writing Exam Overview

The Writing section of ISE Foundation consists of two tasks completed in approximately 70 minutes (30 minutes for Task 3, 40 minutes for Task 4). These tasks test your ability to write clear, well-organised texts at A2 level.

Key Facts:
  • Total time: 70 minutes for both tasks
  • Total word count: 140-200 words (70-100 per task)
  • Text types: Essay, article, email, letter, review
  • Subject areas: Holidays, shopping, school/work, hobbies, food, weekend activities, jobs, places, home life, weather, free time, dates, natural world
Task 3: Reading into Writing 70-100 words

Time advised: 30 minutes

Source material: Use information from Task 2 (Multi-text Reading)

Must include: 3 content points from the question

Key skill: Paraphrasing (using your own words, not copying)

Task 4: Extended Writing 70-100 words

Time advised: 40 minutes

Source material: Use your own ideas and experience

Must include: 2 content points from the question

Key skill: Organising your own ideas coherently

Assessment Criteria

Both tasks are assessed on the same criteria:

Criterion What the Examiner Looks For
Task Fulfilment Did you include all required content points? Is the word count correct?
Text Organisation Clear paragraphs, logical order, appropriate opening and closing
Language Control Grammar accuracy, vocabulary range, spelling, punctuation
Register & Format Appropriate style (formal/informal) and text type features
Important: You must write between 70-100 words. If you write fewer than 70 or more than 100 words, you will lose marks!
Task 3: Reading into Writing (30 minutes)

Word count: 70-100 words

Source: Information from Task 2 texts (3-4 short texts)

Content points: 3 bullet points to include

Key skill: Paraphrasing (don't copy from texts!)

Step-by-Step Approach

Step 1: Analyze the Question (2 minutes)
  • What text type? (essay, article, email, review)
  • Who is the reader?
  • What are the 3 content points?
Step 2: Select Information from Texts (5 minutes)
  • Go back to Task 2 texts
  • Find information for each content point
  • Make notes (don't copy full sentences)
Step 3: Plan Your Text (3 minutes)
  • Introduction: 1 sentence to introduce topic
  • Body: 2-3 sentences covering the 3 content points
  • Conclusion: 1 sentence (opinion or summary)
Step 4: Write (15 minutes)
  • Use your own words - paraphrase!
  • Include all 3 content points
  • Use simple connectors: and, but, because, so
Step 5: Check (5 minutes)
  • Word count (70-100 words)
  • All content points included?
  • Spelling and grammar
Task 3 Sample with Model Answer
Task 2 Texts Summary:
Text A: Maria went to Spain with family, stayed in beach hotel, sunny weather, went swimming, expensive but worth it
Text B: Tom went hiking in Scotland with friend, stayed in hostels, rainy weather, walked in mountains, cheap, felt fit
Text C: Johnson family went to Paris, stayed in city centre apartment, crowded, visited Eiffel Tower and museums, travelled by Eurostar
Task 3 Question:
Write an article (70-100 words) for your school magazine about holidays. Use the information you read in Task 2 to:
  • describe different types of holidays people had
  • say what the weather was like
  • say how people travelled
Model Answer (85 words):

Different Types of Holidays

People enjoy different types of holidays. Maria went to Spain with her family and stayed in a hotel by the beach. The weather was hot and sunny every day. She travelled there by plane. Tom preferred hiking in Scotland with his friend. The weather wasn't good and it rained a lot. He went by train. The Johnson family visited Paris and stayed in an apartment. They travelled by Eurostar. In my opinion, beach holidays are the best because I love swimming in the sea.
Analysis:
  • Content point 1: Different holidays (beach, hiking, city) ✓
  • Content point 2: Weather (sunny, rainy) ✓
  • Content point 3: Transport (plane, train, Eurostar) ✓
  • Paraphrasing: "hot and sunny" instead of "fantastic - sunny and hot", "wasn't good" instead of "wasn't great"
  • Own words: No copied sentences from texts
  • Word count: 85 words (within 70-100 range)
Common Mistakes in Task 3
Mistake Why It's Wrong How to Avoid
Copying from texts Shows no paraphrasing skill Change vocabulary and sentence structure
Missing content points Loses marks for task fulfilment Check off each bullet point as you write
Adding own ideas Must only use information from texts Stick to facts from Task 2
Wrong word count Penalised if under 70 or over 100 Count words and adjust
Task 4: Extended Writing (40 minutes)

Word count: 70-100 words

Source: Your own ideas and experience

Content points: 2 bullet points to include

Key skill: Organising your own ideas clearly

Step-by-Step Approach

Step 1: Analyze the Question (5 minutes)
  • What text type? (email, letter, article, essay, review)
  • Who are you writing to?
  • What are the 2 content points?
  • Formal or informal register?
Step 2: Brainstorm Ideas (5 minutes)
  • Think of vocabulary related to the topic
  • Decide on specific examples to include
  • Plan your opinion (if asked)
Step 3: Plan Your Text (5 minutes)
  • Opening: Greeting + purpose (for emails/letters) or title + introduction
  • Middle: Cover both content points with examples
  • Closing: Appropriate ending for text type
Step 4: Write (20 minutes)
  • Write clearly and simply
  • Use connectors: and, but, because, so, then
  • Include specific details (names, places, times)
Step 5: Check (5 minutes)
  • Word count (70-100 words)
  • Both content points included?
  • Appropriate opening and closing?
  • Spelling and grammar checked?
Task 4 Sample Questions with Model Answers

Sample 1: Informal Email to a Friend

Task: A friend from another country wants to know about your home. Write an email (70-100 words) to your friend. You should:
  • describe your home and
  • say what you like doing there.
Model Answer (78 words):

Hi Sam,

Thanks for your email. I'm writing to tell you about my home. I live in a flat with my parents and my sister. It's not very big but it's comfortable. We have two bedrooms, a living room and a small kitchen.

I really like spending time in my bedroom because it's my own space. I listen to music and chat with friends online there. At the weekend, we watch films together in the living room.

Write soon!
Maria
Why this is good:
  • Appropriate email format (greeting, message, closing)
  • Content point 1: Describes home (flat, rooms) ✓
  • Content point 2: Says what she likes doing (bedroom activities, watching films) ✓
  • Informal register (Hi, Thanks, Write soon)
  • Word count: 78 words ✓

Sample 2: Article for Magazine

Task: Your town has a magazine with information about places people can visit. Write an article (70-100 words) for the magazine about a park in your town. You should:
  • describe the park and
  • say what you can do there and who goes there.
Model Answer (82 words):

Riverside Park: A Great Place to Visit

Riverside Park is the most beautiful place in our town. It is next to the river and has lots of green grass and tall trees. There are colourful flowers everywhere in spring and summer.

You can do many activities there. There is a playground for children and a skate park for teenagers. People can walk their dogs or have picnics on the grass. Families, students and old people all enjoy this park. It is the perfect place to relax and have fun.
Why this is good:
  • Title included (article format)
  • Content point 1: Describes park (location, appearance) ✓
  • Content point 2: Activities and who goes there ✓
  • Descriptive vocabulary (beautiful, colourful, perfect)
  • Word count: 82 words ✓

Sample 3: Review

Task: Last week you went on holiday and stayed in a hotel. Write a review (70-100 words) of the hotel for a holiday website. You should:
  • describe the hotel and
  • say what is good and bad about the hotel.
Model Answer (88 words):

Hotel Review: The Seaview Hotel

I stayed at The Seaview Hotel last week with my family. It is a large hotel next to the beach with a swimming pool and two restaurants. Our room was clean and comfortable with a beautiful view of the sea.

The best thing about this hotel is the friendly staff. They were always helpful and smiling. The breakfast was also fantastic with lots of fresh fruit. However, the Wi-Fi was very slow and the hotel was quite expensive.

I recommend this hotel for families who want a relaxing beach holiday.
Why this is good:
  • Title included (review format)
  • Content point 1: Describes hotel (location, facilities, room) ✓
  • Content point 2: Good points (staff, breakfast) and bad points (Wi-Fi, price) ✓
  • Recommendation at the end
  • Word count: 88 words ✓
Text Types for ISE Foundation Writing

1. Email (Informal)

Feature Example
Greeting Hi [name], / Hello [name],
Opening Thanks for your email. / How are you? / I'm writing to...
Closing Write soon. / See you soon. / Bye for now.
Sign-off Best wishes, / Love, / From,

2. Letter (Formal)

Feature Example
Greeting Dear Mr/Ms [surname], / Dear Sir/Madam,
Opening I am writing to... / I would like to...
Closing I look forward to hearing from you.
Sign-off Yours sincerely, / Yours faithfully,

3. Article (Magazine or Online)

Feature Example
Title Catchy and interesting: "My Favourite Place" / "The Best Cafe in Town"
Introduction General statement about the topic
Body Information, description, examples
Conclusion Opinion or recommendation
Language Engaging, descriptive adjectives

4. Review

Feature Example
Title Name of place/film/book: "Restaurant Review: Pizza Palace"
Introduction When you went/saw it, with who
Description What it was like (location, appearance, atmosphere)
Evaluation Good points and bad points
Recommendation I recommend... / I suggest... / Perfect for...

5. Essay (Descriptive)

Feature Example
Introduction Introduce the topic
Body paragraphs Description with details and examples
Conclusion Personal opinion or summary
Language Adjectives and adverbs for description
Formal vs Informal Language
Informal (Emails to friends) Formal (Letters to officials)
Hi / Hello Dear Mr/Ms...
Thanks Thank you
I'm / You're / It's I am / You are / It is
Great / Awesome / Fantastic Very good / Excellent
Bye / See you Yours sincerely / faithfully
Can you...? Could you...? / I would like...
Writing Strategies for ISE Foundation

Grammar at A2 Level

You should demonstrate:
  • Present Simple: I live, She works, Do you like?
  • Present Continuous: I am writing, She is studying
  • Past Simple: I visited, She went, Did you see?
  • Future with going to: I'm going to visit
  • Can / Can't: I can swim, She can't come
  • Comparatives: bigger, more expensive
  • Connectors: and, but, because, so, then

Useful Phrases for Writing

Opening phrases:
• I'm writing to tell you about...
• Thanks for your email.
• I would like to tell you about...
• This article is about...
Adding information:
• and
• also
• as well
• too (at the end of sentence)
Contrasting:
• but
• however
• although
Giving reasons:
• because
• so
• that's why
Closing phrases:
• I hope to hear from you soon.
• Write soon.
• I recommend this place because...
• In my opinion,...
Final Checklist Before Submitting
  • Word count is between 70-100 words
  • All content points from the question are included
  • Appropriate opening and closing for text type
  • Correct register (formal or informal)
  • Paragraphs are clear and logical
  • Used connectors (and, but, because)
  • Checked spelling of common words
  • Checked punctuation (full stops, commas, capital letters)
  • Checked verb tenses (past for past events, present for routines)
  • Handwriting is clear and easy to read (or typed neatly)
Time Management in the Exam
Activity Task 3 (30 mins) Task 4 (40 mins)
Analyze question 2 minutes 5 minutes
Plan / Brainstorm 8 minutes (select from texts) 10 minutes
Write 15 minutes 20 minutes
Check 5 minutes 5 minutes
Important: Always leave time to check your work! Many mistakes can be caught and corrected in the final 5 minutes.
