Task 3: Reading into Writing & Task 4: Extended Writing

Writing Module Overview

The ISE I Writing module consists of two tasks completed in 80 minutes (40 minutes per task). These tasks test your ability to write different text types at B1 level, using information from reading texts (Task 3) and your own ideas (Task 4).

Key Information:
  • Total Time: 80 minutes (40 mins per task - strict limit)
  • Word Count: 100-130 words per task
  • Total Marks: 28 marks (combined across both tasks)
  • Pass Mark: 14/28 (50%)
  • Merit: 20/28
  • Distinction: 25/28

Task 3: Reading into Writing 40 minutes 100-130 words
Task Overview: Write a text (100-130 words) using information from the 4 texts in Task 2. You must address 3 bullet points from the question and use your own words - copying from the texts will result in penalties.

Sample Task 3 Question:

Write an article (100-130 words) for a school or college magazine saying what you think is the best way to get fit.

Use the information you read in texts A, B, C and D (pages 4 and 5) to:
¬ª explain which types of exercise are best for getting fit
¬ª say what equipment you need to have for each activity and
¬ª talk about a recent trend in exercise.

Do not copy from the texts. Use your own words as far as possible.

Step-by-Step Approach:

1 Analyze the Question (5 minutes)
  • Identify the genre (article, essay, email, letter, review)
  • Identify the 3 bullet points you must address
  • Note the target reader (school magazine, teacher, friend)
  • Determine register (formal or informal)
2 Scan the 4 Texts for Information (10 minutes)

Go back to Task 2 texts and find relevant information for each bullet point:

Bullet 1: Types of exercise best for fitness ‚Üí Text A (swimming), Text B (cycling), Text C (gym)
Bullet 2: Equipment needed ‚Üí Text A (swimming costume, cap, goggles), Text C (gym equipment)
Bullet 3: Recent trend ‚Üí Text D (fall in physical activity since 1965)
3 Plan Your Structure (5 minutes)
  • Introduction (20-25 words): Hook the reader, introduce topic
  • Body Paragraph 1 (30-35 words): Address bullet point 1 with evidence from texts
  • Body Paragraph 2 (30-35 words): Address bullet point 2 with evidence from texts
  • Conclusion (20-25 words): Address bullet point 3 + closing thought
4 Write Your Answer (15 minutes)
  • Use your own words - paraphrase the text information
  • Include information from all 4 texts
  • Use linking words (furthermore, however, in addition)
  • Check word count as you write (aim for 110-125)
5 Check and Revise (5 minutes)
  • Have you addressed all 3 bullet points?
  • Have you used information from all 4 texts?
  • Is the word count 100-130?
  • Check spelling, grammar, and punctuation

Sample Model Answer (Article):

The Best Way to Get Fit

There are many ways to get fit, but some exercises are more effective than others. Swimming is excellent because it exercises all your muscles and is less damaging to joints than running. Cycling is also good, especially in parks, though you need to be careful of pedestrians. If you prefer indoor activities, the gym offers various options like running machines and weights.

The equipment you need depends on your choice. For swimming, you only need a costume, cap and goggles. For the gym, you don't need to buy equipment as it's provided. Cycling requires a bicycle and helmet.

Unfortunately, studies show that physical activity has fallen since 1965, and fewer people will exercise in 2030 than in 2015. We need to reverse this trend!

(128 words)
‚úì What makes this answer good:
  • All 3 bullet points addressed clearly
  • Information from all 4 texts included
  • Own words used - no copying from texts
  • Article format with title and engaging opening
  • Within word count (128 words)
  • Clear paragraph structure

Task 4: Extended Writing 40 minutes 100-130 words
Task Overview: Write a text (100-130 words) using your own ideas - no reading texts are provided. You must address 2 bullet points from the question. This tests your ability to write independently.

Sample Task 4 Questions by Genre:

Essay:
Write an essay (100-130 words) for your teacher about a festival in a country you know. You should:
¬ª give examples of what happens at this festival and
¬ª explain why this festival is important.
Email to a Friend:
Write an email (100-130 words) to a friend about the rules at your college. You should:
¬ª describe some of the rules and
¬ª explain what you think of these rules.
Review:
Write a review (100-130 words) of a restaurant you visited recently for a travel website. You should:
¬ª describe the food and service and
¬ª say whether you would recommend it to others.

Step-by-Step Approach:

1 Analyze the Question (5 minutes)
  • Identify the genre (article, essay, email, letter, review)
  • Identify the 2 bullet points you must address
  • Brainstorm ideas from your own knowledge/experience
  • Plan your paragraph structure
2 Plan Your Content (5 minutes)

Make brief notes for each paragraph:

Introduction: Introduce topic + your main idea
Body: Address bullet point 1 with examples
Body: Address bullet point 2 with your opinion
Conclusion: Summarize + final thought
3 Write Your Answer (20 minutes)
  • Use appropriate genre features (see Genres section)
  • Include personal opinions and experiences
  • Use B1 language functions (conditionals, modals, passives)
  • Check word count regularly
4 Check and Revise (10 minutes)
  • Have you addressed both bullet points?
  • Is the word count 100-130?
  • Check spelling, grammar, and punctuation
  • Verify genre features (title, greeting, closing)

Sample Model Answer (Essay):

Diwali: The Festival of Lights

Diwali is one of the most important festivals in India, celebrated every autumn. During this festival, people decorate their homes with oil lamps and colourful lights. Families prepare special sweets and share them with neighbours and friends. Many people also buy new clothes and exchange gifts with their loved ones.

This festival is important because it represents the victory of light over darkness and good over evil. It brings families together and strengthens community bonds. Furthermore, it is a time when people forgive past conflicts and start fresh relationships. In my opinion, Diwali teaches us valuable lessons about hope and unity that are relevant to everyone, regardless of their background.

(125 words)
Text Genres & Their Features

Both Task 3 and Task 4 may require you to write in one of these genres. Each has specific features you must include:

üìù Article

Purpose: Inform or persuade readers of a magazine/website

Features:

  • Title/headline
  • Engaging opening
  • Direct address to reader ("you")
  • Personal opinion
  • Informal or semi-formal register

Example opening: "Have you ever wondered why exercise is so important?"

üìö Essay

Purpose: Present arguments and opinions formally

Features:

  • Title
  • Formal register (no contractions)
  • Clear thesis statement
  • Logical argumentation
  • Conclusion summarizing points

Example opening: "This essay will discuss the benefits of regular exercise for students."

üìß Email (Informal)

Purpose: Communicate with friends/family

Features:

  • Greeting: "Hi [Name]," or "Hello [Name],"
  • Informal language
  • Contractions (I'm, don't, can't)
  • Phrasal verbs
  • Closing: "Best wishes," "Take care," "See you soon,"

Example opening: "Hi Sarah, How are you? I'm writing to tell you about..."

‚úâÔ∏è Letter (Formal)

Purpose: Communicate officially or professionally

Features:

  • Greeting: "Dear Sir/Madam," or "Dear Mr/Ms [Surname],"
  • Formal vocabulary
  • No contractions
  • Clear purpose stated early
  • Closing: "Yours faithfully," (if Dear Sir/Madam) or "Yours sincerely," (if named)

Example opening: "Dear Sir/Madam, I am writing to complain about..."

‚≠ê Review

Purpose: Evaluate and recommend (or not) something

Features:

  • Title mentioning the item reviewed
  • Description of what it is
  • Positive and negative aspects
  • Specific examples
  • Clear recommendation

Example opening: "Last weekend I visited 'Bella Pasta' restaurant in the city centre."

üì± Email (Formal/Neutral)

Purpose: Professional but less formal than letter

Features:

  • Greeting: "Dear [Name],"
  • Neutral register
  • Some contractions acceptable
  • Clear subject reference
  • Closing: "Best regards," "Kind regards,"

Example opening: "Dear Mr Johnson, Thank you for your email regarding..."

Register: Formal vs Informal
Feature Formal Informal
Contractions Do not use (cannot, will not, I am) Use freely (can't, won't, I'm)
Phrasal verbs Avoid (use Latin-based verbs) Common (get up, put off, turn up)
Vocabulary "assist," "purchase," "sufficient" "help," "buy," "enough"
Sentence structure Complex sentences, passive voice Simple sentences, active voice
Personal pronouns Imersonal ("One should...") Personal ("I think," "You should")
Quick Guide to Register by Genre:
  • Formal: Essay, Formal Letter, Formal Email, Report
  • Informal: Email to friend, Informal Letter
  • Semi-formal/Neutral: Article, Review, Neutral Email

Writing Strategies for Success

Time Management (40 minutes per task):

Phase Time Activity
Planning 10 minutes Analyze question, brainstorm ideas, plan structure
Writing 20 minutes Write the text, check word count regularly
Checking 10 minutes Revise, check bullet points, spelling, grammar
Never skip the planning phase! A well-planned answer writes faster and scores higher than a rushed, disorganized one.

Useful Linking Words & Phrases:

Adding Information

Furthermore, In addition, Moreover, Also, Besides

Contrasting

However, On the other hand, Although, While, Whereas

Giving Examples

For example, For instance, Such as, In particular

Concluding

In conclusion, To sum up, Overall, In summary

B1 Language Functions to Include:

  • Conditionals: "If you exercise regularly, you will feel healthier."
  • Modals: "You should try to eat more vegetables." / "It might rain tomorrow."
  • Passive voice: "The festival is celebrated every year."
  • Comparatives: "Swimming is more relaxing than running."
  • Present perfect: "I have lived here for five years."
Final Checklist Before Submitting
  • Word count is 100-130 words (write the count at the end)
  • All bullet points from the question are addressed
  • Task 3: Information from all 4 texts included
  • Task 3: Information paraphrased (not copied)
  • Appropriate genre features (title, greeting, closing)
  • Consistent register (formal or informal)
  • Clear paragraph structure (introduction, body, conclusion)
  • Linking words used to connect ideas
  • Spelling checked (especially common words)
  • Punctuation checked (full stops, commas, apostrophes)
  • Grammar checked (tenses, articles, prepositions)
  • Handwriting is legible (if handwritten)
