ISE I Portfolio Complete Guide

Understanding Tasks, Requirements, Assessment & Submission

What is the ISE I Portfolio?

The Portfolio is a collection of your best written work that you prepare before the exam. It accounts for 20% of your final grade and allows you to demonstrate your writing ability without the pressure of exam conditions.

Key Characteristics:
  • Learner-led: You choose tasks from the official Trinity list
  • Resource-rich: Use dictionaries, grammar books, online resources, word processors
  • Process-oriented: Draft, edit, and revise until satisfied
  • Authentic: Write about real topics that interest you
  • Integrated: Tasks relate to the 12 ISE I subject areas
3 Tasks Required

One from each section: Correspondence, Factual Writing, Creative/Descriptive Writing

Word Count Ranges

Section 1: 70-80 words
Section 2: 110-130 words
Section 3: 110-130 words

Preparation Time

Trinity recommends 6-12 weeks of preparation. Work at your own pace.

Teacher Feedback

One feedback form per task only. Teachers cannot correct your work directly.

Section 1: Correspondence 70-80 words
Task Types: Personal letters, informal/formal emails, notes

Sample Task 1:

Task: A radio station has asked your class to present a short music programme. Write a letter to the radio station saying which songs your class has chosen, why you have chosen these songs and if you think the programme will be popular. 70-80 words

Sample Task 2:

Task: Last year you travelled a long way to your school or job every day. Write an email to a friend describing how you had to travel there, saying what you did while you were travelling and giving your opinion about this experience. 70-80 words

Sample Task 3:

Task: You have recently won two cinema tickets for a new film in a competition and want to invite your friend. Write an email to a friend explaining what you had to do in the competition, what film you want to see and if you think your friend will enjoy it. 70-80 words
Key Features for Correspondence:
  • Appropriate greeting (Dear..., Hi..., Hello...)
  • Clear purpose stated early
  • Informal register for friends (contractions, phrasal verbs)
  • Formal register for official letters (no contractions, formal vocabulary)
  • Appropriate closing (Best wishes, Yours sincerely, Take care)
Section 2: Factual Writing 110-130 words
Task Types: Articles, reports, reviews

Sample Task 1 (Article):

Task: You recently performed in a concert. Write an article for a music magazine describing what you did at the concert and saying what you had to do to prepare. Give your opinion of the concert. 110-130 words

Sample Task 2 (Report):

Task: Write a report for your English class about language-learning activities. Say what activities you have to do to learn a foreign language and what activities you found unhelpful. Say what activities you might do in the future to improve your language skills. 110-130 words

Sample Task 3 (Review):

Task: Write a review for a travel magazine of a city you have visited in the last year. Give your impressions of the accommodation and tourist attractions. Say how long you think visitors to the city need to spend there. 110-130 words
Key Features for Factual Writing:
  • Articles: Title, engaging opening, factual information, personal opinion, direct address to reader
  • Reports: Clear headings, objective information, structured paragraphs, recommendations
  • Reviews: Description of item, balanced evaluation, specific examples, recommendation
Section 3: Creative/Descriptive Writing 110-130 words
Task Types: Stories, diary entries, descriptions

Sample Task 1 (Diary):

Task: Your friend has just become a vegetarian. Write your diary explaining why your friend has become a vegetarian, giving your opinion of your friend's decision and saying if you think more people will become vegetarians in the future. 110-130 words

Sample Task 2 (Story):

Task: Write a story for a writing competition called 'An Interesting Person'. Say what you were doing when you met this person, explain why the person was interesting and say if you might meet this person again. 110-130 words

Sample Task 3 (Description):

Task: An older person showed you a photograph of a birthday party from when he/she was a teenager. Write a description for a teenage magazine saying what the young people in the photograph were wearing and giving your opinion of these clothes. Say what you prefer wearing to parties. 110-130 words
Key Features for Creative/Descriptive Writing:
  • Stories: Clear narrative arc, setting, characters, dialogue (optional), resolution
  • Diary entries: Date, informal register, personal feelings, reflections
  • Descriptions: Sensory details, vivid adjectives, spatial organization, comparisons
Word Count Rules (Critical!)
⚠️ WARNING: Any text over the stated maximum word length range will NOT be marked by the examiner and will affect the grade awarded.
Section Word Count What Counts What Does NOT Count
Section 1: Correspondence 70-80 words All words in the body Addresses, dates, subject lines
Section 2: Factual Writing 110-130 words All words in the text Title, headings, quotations
Section 3: Creative Writing 110-130 words All words in the text Title, story starter (if provided), date (diary)
✓ Best Practice: Always write the word count at the end of each task. Use your word processor's word count tool or count manually. Stay within the range - being under is better than being over!
How the Portfolio is Assessed

The portfolio is assessed by a Trinity examiner using four main criteria. Understanding these criteria helps you focus your efforts:

Criterion Weight What Examiners Look For
Task Fulfilment 25% All parts of task completed, appropriate format, relevant content, word count within range
Organisation 25% Clear paragraphs, logical flow, appropriate openings/closings, cohesive devices
Language Control 25% Grammar accuracy, vocabulary range, B1 structures (conditionals, passives, modals), spelling
Appropriacy 25% Correct register (formal/informal), style suitable for genre, effect on target reader
Sample Assessment Grade C (Pass):
"This is a satisfactory execution of the task, with the main points covered. The task is within the stated word length range at 136 words. The text has not been divided into paragraphs and as a consequence is quite difficult to follow. The required functions are covered (talking about events in the indefinite and recent past and giving reasons). There is a satisfactory level of accuracy and appropriacy with the grammatical, lexical and functional items of the level. However, the range is quite restricted."
Task Weighting & Marks
Important: The three portfolio tasks do NOT have equal weighting. Section 1 (Correspondence) carries less weight than Sections 2 and 3.
Task Section Word Count Weighting Maximum Marks
Section 1: Correspondence 70-80 words 20% 4 marks
Section 2: Factual Writing 110-130 words 40% 8 marks
Section 3: Creative/Descriptive 110-130 words 40% 8 marks
TOTAL - 100% 20 marks
Pass Requirements:
  • To pass the Portfolio component: minimum 10 out of 20 marks
  • Merit: 14 out of 20 marks
  • Distinction: 17 out of 20 marks
Understanding Grades
Grade Description Characteristics
A (Distinction) Excellent All requirements met with sophistication, wide range of language, excellent organisation, minimal errors
B (Merit) Very Good All requirements met well, good range of language, well organised, few errors
C (Pass) Satisfactory Main requirements met, adequate range, satisfactory organisation, some errors but meaning clear
D (Borderline) Weak Some requirements met, limited range, organisation issues, errors may impede meaning
E (Fail) Does not meet criteria Major requirements missing, very limited range, poor organisation, errors impede understanding
U (Ungraded) Academic dishonesty Plagiarism detected - zero marks awarded
Step-by-Step Preparation Process

Week 1-2: Task Selection

Review all 15 tasks (5 per section) from the official Trinity list. Choose tasks that:

  • Interest you personally
  • You have knowledge/experience about
  • Allow you to demonstrate B1 language functions

Tip: Attempt more than 3 tasks initially so you can select your best work.

Week 3-4: First Drafts

Write first drafts of your chosen tasks. Focus on:

  • Answering all parts of the prompt
  • Getting your ideas down
  • Rough word count (don't worry about exact limits yet)

Week 5: Teacher Feedback

Submit drafts to your teacher for feedback using the official form:

  • One feedback form per task ONLY
  • Teacher will tick relevant comments
  • No direct corrections on your text

Week 6-8: Redrafting

Study feedback carefully and rewrite:

  • Address all points on feedback form
  • Check word count carefully
  • Improve grammar and vocabulary
  • Refine organisation and paragraphs

Week 9-10: Final Editing

Polish your work to final standard:

  • Proofread for spelling errors
  • Check punctuation carefully
  • Verify word counts (write at end of each task)
  • Ensure consistent formatting

Week 11-12: Final Preparation

Prepare for submission:

  • Complete Portfolio Cover Sheet
  • Attach Student Portfolio Feedback Forms
  • Organize in correct order: Section 1, 2, 3
  • Place in Trinity colour-coded file

B1 Language Functions to Demonstrate

Your portfolio should showcase these B1 language functions:

Function Example Structures Example Usage
Talking about past events Past simple, Present perfect "I visited Paris last year" / "I've recently started..."
Expressing future plans Going to, Present continuous, Would like to "I'm going to study medicine" / "I'd like to travel..."
Expressing opinions I think..., In my opinion..., I believe... "In my opinion, this is the best approach..."
Giving reasons Because, The reason is..., That's why... "I chose this because it offers more flexibility"
Making comparisons More/less than, -er than, Not as...as "This option is more expensive than the other"
Expressing certainty/uncertainty Definitely, Probably, Might, May "It will probably rain tomorrow" / "I might go..."
Describing processes First, Then, Next, Finally "First, you need to prepare the ingredients"
Making suggestions Why don't we..., How about..., I suggest... "Why don't we meet at 6pm?"

Submission Checklist

Before submitting your portfolio, ensure you have:

  • Three tasks only (one from each section)
  • Each task from the current year's official Trinity list
  • Task question number written on each piece of work
  • Word count written at the end of each task
  • All word counts within the specified ranges
  • Portfolio Cover Sheet completed and attached
  • Student Portfolio Feedback Form attached to each task
  • Tasks ordered: Section 1, Section 2, Section 3
  • Work placed in Trinity colour-coded file
  • No drafts included (final versions only)
  • No corrected versions with teacher's marks
  • Teacher's signature on Cover Sheet
  • Submitted at least one day before examiner's visit
What Happens After Submission?
1 Examiner Assessment

The Trinity examiner assesses your portfolio before or during the Interview component. They use the four criteria: Task Fulfilment, Organisation, Language Control, and Appropriacy.

2 Interview Discussion

During your Speaking exam, the examiner will discuss your portfolio with you (2 minutes). Be prepared to:

  • Explain what you wrote and why
  • Discuss challenges you faced
  • Talk about what you learned
  • Ask the examiner a question about your work
3 Results

You will NOT receive portfolio results on the day of the Interview. Results are sent to your centre via the Results Summary Slip, normally within 6-8 weeks of completing all components.

4 Quality Assurance

A 10% sample of portfolios is photocopied and sent to Trinity's London office for quality assurance and moderation.

Critical Rules & Regulations
⚠️ ZERO TOLERANCE: Breaking these rules will result in a U (Ungraded) grade (zero marks) for the portfolio or even disqualification.

Absolute Prohibitions:

  • Plagiarism: Copying text from any source and presenting it as your own
  • Teacher correction: Teachers must NOT correct, add to, or remove anything from your final task
  • Mixed levels: Tasks must be from ISE I only - not mixed with ISE 0 or ISE II tasks
  • Wrong year: Tasks must be from the current examination year (January-December)
  • More than 3 tasks: Submit exactly 3 tasks - no more, no less
  • Drafts included: Do not include draft versions in final submission

Word Count Penalties:

Exceeding maximum word count: Any text over the stated maximum word length range will NOT be marked and will affect your grade. The examiner stops reading at the word limit.

Acceptable Support:

✓ You CAN use:
  • Dictionaries and grammar reference books
  • Online resources and encyclopedias
  • Word processors with spell-check
  • One feedback form per task from your teacher
  • Quotations (clearly marked and attributed)
Consequences of Rule Breaking

Best Practice Tips
1 Start Early

Begin portfolio work 10-12 weeks before your exam date. Rushing leads to mistakes and lower quality.

2 Attempt Extra Tasks

Try 4-5 tasks initially, then select your best 3 for submission. This gives you options.

3 Use Technology

Use word processors with spell-check. But don't rely solely on them - proofread manually too.

4 Check Word Count Carefully

Leave 10% buffer below maximum. If limit is 130 words, aim for 115-125 to be safe.

5 Keep It Your Own

Never copy from the internet, textbooks, or other students. Examiners can detect plagiarism.

6 Prepare for Discussion

Remember what you wrote! The examiner will ask about your portfolio in the Speaking test.
