About GESE Grade 7 Exam
GESE Grade 7 (CEFR B2.1) represents the entry point to upper-intermediate level. At this stage, you must demonstrate the ability to interact with a degree of fluency, take initiative in conversations, and handle unfamiliar situations. The exam takes 15 minutes and consists of three phases [^7^].

Language Functions at Grade 7 [^7^]:
  • Giving advice and highlighting advantages/disadvantages
  • Making suggestions
  • Describing past habits (used to/would)
  • Expressing possibility and uncertainty
  • Eliciting further information and expansion of ideas
  • Expressing agreement and disagreement
Key Grammar for Grade 7 [^7^]:
  • Second conditional (If I were you, I would...)
  • Simple passive voice
  • Used to / Would for past habits
  • Relative clauses (defining and non-defining)
  • Modals for advice: should, ought to, could, you'd better
  • Modals for possibility: may, might, I'm not sure
  • Discourse connectors: because of, due to

Interactive Task Strategy

Remember to [^7^]:

  • Ask follow-up questions to get more information ("Could you tell me more about...?")
  • Make comments on the examiner's responses ("That sounds challenging")
  • Invite the examiner's opinion ("What do you think about...?")
  • Give advice using second conditional ("If I were him, I would...")
  • Express possibility/uncertainty ("He might find that...", "I'm not sure whether...")
