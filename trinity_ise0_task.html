ISE Foundation Exam Overview (A2)

ISE Foundation is a four-skills exam (Reading, Writing, Speaking, Listening) at CEFR A2 level. The exam is taken in two modules and demonstrates your ability to use English in real-life situations.

Task 1: Long Reading (30 minutes)
What you do: Read one long text (about 300 words, 5 paragraphs) and answer 15 questions.

Question Types (15 questions total):

  • Questions 1-5: Multiple choice - choose the correct answer (A, B, C)
  • Questions 6-10: True/False/Not Given statements
  • Questions 11-15: Complete sentences with exact words from the text
Sample Question Types:
‚Ä¢ Multiple Choice: "What is the main purpose of the text?"
‚Ä¢ True/False: "The writer believes that... [T/F/Not Given]"
‚Ä¢ Gap-fill: "The writer suggests that exercise is _______ for mental health."
Strategy Tips:
  • Read the questions first, then scan for answers
  • For True/False: If the text doesn't mention it, choose "Not Given"
  • For gap-fill: Use the exact words from the text (1-3 words usually)
  • Manage your time: 2 minutes per question maximum
Task 2: Multi-text Reading (30 minutes)
What you do: Read 3-4 short texts on the same topic and answer 15 questions.

Question Types:

  • Questions 16-20: Match descriptions to texts (which text matches?)
  • Questions 21-25: Choose 5 true statements from 8 options (3 are false)
  • Questions 26-30: Complete notes with exact words from texts (choose from list)
Sample Task:
You read 4 short texts about different people's holidays.
Q16: "Which person went on holiday with their family?" ‚Üí Match to Text A/B/C/D
Q21: "Choose 5 true statements: The weather was good / It was expensive / They went swimming..." etc.
Important: The information you use in Task 3 (Reading into Writing) comes from these texts. Pay attention to facts, dates, names, and opinions.
Reading Strategies for A2
Strategy How to Apply
Skimming Read quickly for general idea - look at title, first sentence of each paragraph, conclusion
Scanning Look for specific information - names, dates, numbers, key words from questions
Context clues Guess unknown words from surrounding text - look at the sentence before and after
Keyword matching Find words in the question that appear in the text (but be careful of synonyms)
Task 3: Reading into Writing (30 minutes)
Task Requirements:
  • Write 70-100 words
  • Use information from the 3-4 texts in Task 2
  • Write in your own words - do not copy from the texts
  • Text types: descriptive essay, article, email/letter, or review

How to Approach This Task:

Step 1: Analyze the Question

Identify what you need to write: "Write an article for your school magazine describing different types of holidays people prefer."

Step 2: Select Information from Texts

Go back to Task 2 texts and find relevant facts. Make notes:

  • Text A: Maria likes beach holidays, went to Spain last year
  • Text B: Tom prefers adventure holidays, went hiking in Scotland
  • Text C: The Johnsons enjoy city breaks, visited Paris

Step 3: Plan Your Structure

  • Introduction (1 sentence): Introduce the topic
  • Body (2-3 sentences): Include information from all texts
  • Conclusion (1 sentence): Personal opinion or summary
Sample Answer (85 words):
"People enjoy different types of holidays. Maria loves beach holidays and she went to Spain last summer. In contrast, Tom prefers adventure holidays. He went hiking in the mountains in Scotland. The Johnson family likes city breaks. They visited Paris and saw the Eiffel Tower. In my opinion, beach holidays are the best because I like swimming and relaxing in the sun."
Common Mistakes to Avoid:
  • ‚ùå Copying sentences directly from the reading texts (penalty!)
  • ‚ùå Writing less than 70 words or more than 100 words
  • ‚ùå Not using information from all the texts
  • ‚ùå Forgetting to include your own opinion (if asked)
Task 4: Extended Writing (30 minutes)
Task Requirements:
  • Write 70-100 words
  • No reading texts provided - use your own ideas
  • Text types: descriptive essay, article, email/letter, or review

Text Types Explained:

1. Descriptive Essay

Describe a person, place, or thing. Use adjectives and sensory details.

Example topic: "Describe your favourite place in your town."

2. Article (Magazine or Online)

Informal, interesting title, engaging opening, personal opinion.

Example topic: "Write an article about your hobby for a school magazine."

3. Email or Letter (Informal or Neutral)

Correct opening/closing, clear purpose, appropriate tone.

Example topic: "Write an email to a friend inviting them to visit you."

4. Review

Describe and give opinion about a film, book, restaurant, or place.

Example topic: "Write a review of a restaurant you visited recently."

Sample Extended Writing Tasks with Model Answers

Sample 1: Email to a Friend (Informal)

Task: You are going to visit your friend in another city. Write an email to your friend. Tell them:

  • When you will arrive
  • What you want to do together
  • What you are looking forward to
Model Answer (78 words):
Hi Sarah,

I'm writing to tell you that I'm coming to visit you next weekend. I'll arrive on Saturday morning at about 10 o'clock. I think we can go shopping in the afternoon and then see a film in the evening. On Sunday, I'd like to visit the museum if that's OK with you. I'm really looking forward to seeing you and spending time together. It's been too long!

See you soon,
Maria

Sample 2: Article About a Hobby

Task: Write an article for your school magazine about a hobby you enjoy. Say:

  • What the hobby is
  • When and where you do it
  • Why you enjoy it
Model Answer (82 words):
My Favourite Hobby: Photography

My favourite hobby is photography. I take photos every weekend when I go to the park or the city centre. I usually use my smartphone but sometimes I use my dad's camera. I enjoy photography because I love capturing special moments. Last month, I took amazing photos of my sister's birthday party. I also like sharing my photos on Instagram. In the future, I want to learn how to edit photos properly. Photography helps me see the beauty in everyday life.

Sample 3: Review of a Restaurant

Task: Write a review of a restaurant you visited recently. Include:

  • The name and location of the restaurant
  • What you ate
  • Your opinion of the food and service
Model Answer (76 words):
Restaurant Review: Pizza Palace

Last Saturday, I went to Pizza Palace with my family. It is in the city centre, near the train station. I ordered a margarita pizza and my brother had pasta. The food was delicious and the portions were very big. The waiter was friendly but the service was a bit slow because the restaurant was busy. The prices were reasonable - about ‚Ç¨15 per person. I recommend this restaurant for families. I will definitely go back soon!

Topic Task (4 minutes)
What you do: You prepare a topic before the exam. You bring a Topic Form with 4 points to discuss. The examiner asks you questions about your topic.

How to Choose Your Topic:

  • Choose something you know well and can talk about easily
  • Choose something you are interested in (you'll speak better!)
  • Make sure you can demonstrate A2 language functions
  • Do NOT choose from the Conversation subject areas (Holidays, Shopping, etc.)
Sample Topic Form: "My Favourite Sport - Swimming"
When I started swimming and why
My training schedule (routines)
A competition I participated in (past)
My future swimming goals
Language Functions to Demonstrate:
  • Giving personal information about present and past circumstances
  • Describing routines (present simple)
  • Describing ability and inability (can/can't, could/couldn't)
  • Describing future plans (going to, would like to)
  • Expressing likes and dislikes
  • Describing people, objects and places
  • Expressing simple comparisons (comparative adjectives)
  • Asking for information (asking the examiner a question!)
Sample Exchange:
Examiner: "Can you tell me about your swimming routine?"
You: "I swim three times a week. On Mondays and Wednesdays, I go to the pool after school. I usually swim for one hour. At the weekend, I have longer training sessions on Saturday mornings. I can swim 50 metres in 35 seconds now, but last year I couldn't do it that fast."
Examiner: "What would you like to achieve in the future?"
You: "I'm going to compete in the regional championship next month. I hope I can win a medal. In the future, I would like to become a swimming instructor. Do you enjoy swimming, examiner?"
Conversation Task (2 minutes)
What you do: The examiner chooses one subject area and asks you questions. You need to respond and ask the examiner at least one question.

Subject Areas (Examiner chooses one):

Holidays ‚Äî Past trips, dream destinations, preferences
Shopping ‚Äî Favourite shops, online vs. high street, last purchase
School and work ‚Äî Subjects, future career, daily routine
Hobbies and sports ‚Äî Free time activities, abilities, preferences
Food ‚Äî Cooking, favourite meals, restaurants, healthy eating
Weekend and seasonal activities ‚Äî Saturday routines, summer vs. winter
Sample Conversation about "Food":
Examiner: "Let's talk about food. Can you cook?"
You: "Yes, I can cook simple meals. I make pasta and omelettes. I can't cook complicated dishes yet, but I'm learning. I cook dinner for my family twice a week."
Examiner: "What did you have for breakfast this morning?"
You: "I had toast with jam and a cup of tea. I usually don't eat much in the morning. Do you prefer sweet or savoury breakfast, examiner?"
Important: You MUST ask the examiner a question in this task. Prepare some questions in advance:
  • "Do you like...?"
  • "Have you ever...?"
  • "What do you think about...?"
  • "Do you prefer... or...?"
Speaking Assessment Criteria
Criterion What the Examiner Looks For
Communicative Effectiveness Can you complete the tasks? Do you respond appropriately? Is your message clear?
Interactive Listening Do you understand the examiner? Do you respond to questions naturally? Do you initiate conversation?
Language Control Grammar accuracy (tenses, articles, prepositions), vocabulary range, word order
Delivery Pronunciation, intonation, fluency, speed, clarity
Independent Listening Task (8 minutes)
Overview: Two separate recordings. You listen twice to each. Task 1 requires written answers. Task 2 requires you to report facts and answer questions orally.

Task 1: Listening for Information (Written Response)

  • You see a map, plan, or diagram with pictures
  • You listen to someone describing locations
  • You match letters (B, C, D, etc.) to numbers on the map
  • One picture is not used (distraction)
  • You write your answers on paper
Sample Task 1:
You see a map of an airport waiting room with 5 numbered circles and 6 pictures (A-F).
You hear: "The information desk is on the left side of the room, next to the entrance."
You write the correct letter in circle 1 on your map.
Tip: Listen for directions (left, right, opposite, next to, between) and locations (entrance, exit, corner, centre).

Task 2: Listening for Facts (Oral Response)

  • You listen to a monologue (one person speaking)
  • The recording is approximately 1 minute long
  • You can take notes while listening
  • After listening, the examiner asks you 6 questions
  • You answer in one or two words or short phrases
Sample Task 2:
You hear a woman talking about her weekend.
Examiner asks: "What did she do on Saturday morning?"
You answer: "Went shopping" or "She went shopping."
Examiner asks: "Who did she go with?"
You answer: "Her sister" or "With her sister."
Listening Strategies:
  • First listening: Get the general idea, don't worry about details
  • Second listening: Focus on specific information, write key words
  • For maps: Quickly look at the layout before listening
  • For facts: Note down numbers, names, times, places
  • Don't worry if you miss something - you hear it twice!

Key Information to Listen For (Task 2):

  • Times and dates: "I arrived at 3 o'clock on Saturday."
  • Places: "We went to the beach."
  • People: "I went with my brother."
  • Activities: "We played football and had a picnic."
  • Transport: "We went by bus."
  • Prices: "It cost twenty euros."
  • Opinions: "I thought it was very interesting."
